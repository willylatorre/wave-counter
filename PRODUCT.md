# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Wave Counter serves two people at once. Library adopters are Python, Node, and Vue developers who need a trustworthy counter without rebuilding persistence or interaction details. Their visitors are anonymous people making a quick, low-stakes gesture inside a host site, often on touch devices and often without knowing Wave Counter exists.

## Product Purpose

Wave Counter makes one anonymous event feel immediate while preserving an authoritative global total and useful seven-day context. Success means the component disappears into its host application, the first click feels direct, analytics remain discoverable without crowding the primary action, and framework integrations behave identically.

## Brand Personality

Calm, tactile, quietly playful. The component should create a small moment of satisfaction without asking for attention, adding gamification pressure, or looking like an analytics dashboard.

## Anti-references

- Casino counters, streak mechanics, confetti, badges, and other engagement-pressure patterns.
- Generic SaaS dashboard cards or a mini admin panel attached to a tiny action.
- Neon-on-dark developer-tool styling, purple gradients, glassmorphism, and decorative glow.
- Bouncy, elastic, slow, or non-interruptible motion.
- Coffee-themed brown palettes that make the reusable library look specific to one counter.

## Design Principles

1. **The event is primary.** One press records one event; every other capability stays secondary.
2. **Authority without latency.** Respond immediately, then reconcile visibly but without drama.
3. **Analytics by invitation.** Stats are available through familiar secondary gestures and never compete with the button.
4. **Host-friendly by default.** The component carries a complete visual baseline while yielding cleanly to CSS variables, slots, and surrounding typography.
5. **Craft stays quiet.** Detail, motion, and accessibility should be felt as correctness rather than noticed as decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA for contrast, keyboard operation, focus visibility, target size, and error communication. Every pointer gesture has a keyboard equivalent. Long press must cancel when touch intent becomes scrolling. Text duplicates chart meaning, color is never the only signal, and reduced motion, reduced transparency, increased contrast, and enlarged text retain every state change.
